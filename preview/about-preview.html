About Diane Anderson

The people you serve decide to stay, or drift away, in the moments that matter.

Twenty years of high-stakes service taught me what earns trust and what earns the kind of loyalty that lasts. That experience now works for your mission.

As a Starwood Preferred Guest loyalty program agent, I served guests who had earned their trips point by point. They called to upgrade a room or rescue a travel plan, and every word either built trust or broke it. The program partnered with American Express to give guests a smoother travel experience, and I saw up close what keeps a loyal guest loyal. Your organization needs the same thing from every supporter who says yes once.

At Princess Cruises, I chose the direct customer lines over the travel agent lines. Many of my calls were parents planning to gift their kids a cruise wedding, with the savings becoming a down payment on the couple's life together. I listened for the hope underneath the question. Your supporters carry that same unspoken hope, and content that hears it is content they answer.

For seventeen and a half years at a bank, I supported the executive attorney who managed the bank's litigation and compliance across three states. That was my education in fiduciary duty. People's private matters stay private. From there I went to Sheppard Mullin as client liaison for a trial attorney who represented clients in employment cases, where one wrong word carried real consequences. Your next ask and your hardest update deserve that same precision.

"Communication lives in the trenches. It happens when you show up for people in the moments that matter most."

Then came the caregiving years. I served people who couldn't serve themselves. The disabled. The lost elder orphans who had no one else. In those rooms, I learned what real communication looks like when pretense is gone and all that's left is one human being reaching another. That's the standard every word written for your people has to meet.

Then my chihuahua, Leo Waxman. Blind and deaf, seventeen years old. He reached the moment where love meant letting go. I called six vets. Compassionate end-of-life care started at $350, and I had no idea how to manage it.

Then I found Joybound. They gave me compassion, and they enrolled me in an assistance program so I could give Leo the gentle goodbye he deserved.

That last day stays with me. It was peaceful. It was filled with love.

A few weeks later, I sat down and wrote it straight. The desperation. The kindness. Leo safe in my arms. That letter became my gift back to Joybound, written because they changed everything for me and the one thing I had to give was the story.

That letter is proof of something your organization already owns: a moment where everything shifted for someone you served. You have that story. My job is to find it, and turn it into communication that builds real trust and lasting loyalty with the people who matter most to you.

What I Believe

Story is the secret sauce. It always has been.

Story grounds everything.

Products. Services. Missions. Loyalty programs. None of them land without a story that makes the reader feel something real. Not sentimental. Real.

Meet people where they are.

Your donor is in a different place than your first-time visitor. Your returning client needs something different than your new referral. Content that works knows the difference.

Trust comes before loyalty.

You can't ask for loyalty from someone who doesn't trust you yet. The content we build together earns trust first. Consistently, through real communication. And lets loyalty follow naturally.

Technology is not the secret sauce.

It never was. The secret has always been how we serve the people in front of us. The platforms change. That truth doesn't.
