Twenty years of listening to people say
what matters most.

Communication isn't something I learned in a classroom. I learned it in the trenches — selling and serving in the Starwood Preferred Guest loyalty program, where I saw firsthand what it takes to earn someone's return. Taking direct calls at Princess Cruises, helping families plan the group cruise, the wedding, the anniversary trip they'd been saving toward for years. Then working alongside an executive attorney at a law firm, serving the trial team, acting as liaison to the client — helping them access documents, organizing their answers, and doing the quiet work of creating connection and trust when the stakes were high.

And then caring for real people. The disabled. The lost elder orphans who had no one else. You learn to listen differently when stakes are that human.

Then my chihuahua, Leo Waxman — blind and deaf, seventeen years old — reached the moment where love meant letting go. Joybound enrolled me in their assistance program so I could give him the gentle goodbye he deserved. I wrote them a donor letter as my gift in return. Not because anyone asked. Because they had changed everything for me, and I had the one thing I could give: the ability to tell that story.

That letter is my transformation story. It's also what I do for every client — find the moment where everything shifted, and turn it into the kind of communication that builds real trust and lasting loyalty.
